December is that time when we all collectively relax just a little bit - we know that Christmas is just around the corner and we let ourselves go a little loose. We no longer feel the need to be constantly busy, so we focus more on spending time with friends, and family, for all of this to culminate on Christmas Eve. So in the spirit of the season - please enjoy this selection of carefully tailored events.

## ACTIVITIES

If you love your snowy gore and Scottish tales of horror, feel free to explore **HauntedBurgh** with **tours** for as little as £15. **(Runs every week Wednesday through to Friday)**. [https://hauntedburgh.com/](https://hauntedburgh.com/)

A fascinating lecture on **art history by Sarah E. James.** During the Cold War, the GDR, influenced by the USSR, became a major uranium producer, supplying 220,000 tons exclusively to the Soviet Union. This talk explores how nuclear themes influenced countercultural art and literature in East Germany. Read more here. **(12th of December, 5:15pm)** [https://www.eventbrite.co.uk/e/history-of-art-research-seminar-series-professor-sarah-e-james-tickets-1013738086907?aff=ALLEVENTS](https://www.eventbrite.co.uk/e/history-of-art-research-seminar-series-professor-sarah-e-james-tickets-1013738086907?aff=ALLEVENTS)

And naturally, we cannot forget the **Christmas at the Botanics** with light trails that are always a source of visual entertainment. **(runs until 30th of December, multiple dates, multiple times)** [https://www.rbge.org.uk/whats-on/christmas-at-the-botanics/](https://www.rbge.org.uk/whats-on/christmas-at-the-botanics/)

**Midnight Lunar Market - Holiday Edition** for all your quirky, potentially witchy needs.
