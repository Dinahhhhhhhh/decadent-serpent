Wake of the Red Witch is a 1948 adventure film, set on the high seas, and the tropical jungles of a Caribbean island. While it’s not a pirate movie, it certainly involves theft and underhanded behaviours. It is an American adventure film that falls into the romantic, adventure and melodrama genres.

Directed by [Edward Ludwig](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edward_Ludwig), produced by [Edmund Grainger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edmund_Grainger) and starring [John Wayne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Wayne), [Gail Russell](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gail_Russell), [Gig Young](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Gig_Young), [Adele Mara](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Adele_Mara) and [Luther Adler](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Luther_Adler). 

![](/images/uploads/2025/02/wake-red.png)

The movie is about two men carrying out an ongoing rivalry: Mayrant Sidneye, owner of the shipping company Batjak Limited, and Captain Ralls.

The movie opens aboard the Red Witch just before she sinks. It then cuts to a trial of the captain where he escapes a charge of [barratr](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Barratry_\(admiralty_law\))y, when Batjak unexpectedly withdraws its complaint. Then we proceed to the Carribean Island, where we are met with a backstory about Ralls and Sidneye falling in love with the same beautiful woman, Angelique with further unexpected elements along the way.

The novel _Wake of the Red Witch_ was written by [Garland Roark](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Garland_Roark), a Texan who worked in advertising.The _Washington Post_ called it "a smashing melodrama." It was published in 1946 and became a bestseller.

The film has numerous similarities to [Cecil B. DeMille](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Cecil_B._DeMille)'s earlier seagoing spectacular [_Reap the Wild Wind_](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Reap_the_Wild_Wind) (1942) apart from the titles ("RWW" and "WRW") such as both starring [John Wayne](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/John_Wayne) as a sea captain in two of his few roles as a anti-hero and his similar demise in both films.

[Republic Pictures](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Republic_Pictures) paid $100,000 for the screen rights to the book, reportedly the highest amount that the studio had ever paid. Republic, a [Poverty Row](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Poverty_Row) studio in its early days, primarily produced low- and medium-budget [Westerns](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Western_\(genre\)) and [serials](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Serial_film). [Edmund Grainger](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Edmund_Grainger) was assigned as producer.

The movie is in black and white and is 1hr and 26min long, and would be rated PG. The only spicyness is two or three passionate kisses, and everyone keeps their clothes on. There is little to no bad language.
