***PAISLEY***

**Literature**

Paisley Book Festival — 15–17 May. Ticketed. This year's theme is Everyday Heroes, Everlasting Icons, with appearances from Val McDermid, Jenni Fagan, Doug Johnstone, and Nicola Sturgeon. https://www.paisleybookfestival.co.uk/

**Films**

1–7 May — Filmhouse, Edinburgh

Klute (1971) — Dir. Alan J. Pakula. Jane Fonda and Donald Sutherland in Pakula's neo-noir thriller, part of the Filmhouse Paranoia Trilogy season.

All the President's Men (50th Anniversary, 4K Restoration, 1976) — Dir. Alan J. Pakula. Redford and Hoffman as Woodward and Bernstein.

The Conversation (4K Restoration, 1974) — Dir. Francis Ford Coppola. Gene Hackman as a surveillance expert caught in his own web of paranoia.

4 May — Filmhouse, Edinburgh

Highlander (40th Anniversary, 4K Restoration) — Dir. Russell Mulcahy. With intro and post-screening discussion by author Jonathan Melville.

1–10 May — Cameo, Edinburgh

Folk Film Gathering — The world's first folk film festival returns to the Cameo and Scottish Storytelling Centre. Closes with a screening of Sergei Parajanov's newly remastered feature debut Andriesh (1954, Ukraine), accompanied by a live performance from the Edinburgh Ukrainian Choir. https://www.picturehouses.com/cinema/the-cameo

9–11 May — Filmhouse, Edinburgh

Akira (4K Restoration, 1988) — Dir. Katsuhiro Otomo. Katsuhiro Otomo's landmark cyberpunk anime returns in a new 4K restoration.

16–17 May — Filmhouse, Edinburgh

Sansho the Bailiff (1954) — Dir. Kenji Mizoguchi. An 11th-century tale of slavery and dignity, sharing the Venice Silver Lion in 1954 with Seven Samurai, On the Waterfront, and La Strada.

29 May — Filmhouse, Edinburgh

Portrait of a Lady on Fire (4K Reissue, 2019) — Dir. Céline Sciamma. Winner of the Queer Palm and Best Screenplay at Cannes, reissued in 4K.
